Description:
Abstract:
This ARC/INFO polygon coverage contains the fourth level administrative boundaries at a 1:1000000 scale.

The coverage is part of the Famine Early Warning System (FEWS) / Associates in Rural Development (ARD) African series.

It should be noted that due to the political and social upheaval that has occurred in Ethiopia during the last 20 years it is difficult to trace the exact administrative boundaries. This data set is intended to give the user the best possible political units for the country of Ethiopia.

Ethiopia is associated with the Horn region in Africa.

Purpose:
This data set is to be used in conjunction with other layers of Ethiopia data. The data are intended to show the spatial distribution of the fourth level administrative boundaries for Ethiopia

The data are to be used in modeling, targeting and demonstration project. The data are also used in natural resource management, inventory, change analysis, policy making, environmental awareness and land use planning.

Supplemental_Information:

Procedures_Used: The region boundaries for the country of Ethiopia (prior to the independence of Eritrea on April 27, 1993) were extracted from the "Topographic, Ethiopia" series of maps based on Joint Operations Graphic (JOG) series 1501 and EMA 1:250000 topo series.

Eleven map sheets comprised this series, encompassing three UTM zones. Each sheet was individually digitized and the resulting coverages projected from table coordinates to their appropriate UTM zone. The coverages were then projected to UTM zone 37 (the central zone of the three UTM zones), EDGEMATCHED, and JOINED to produce one country wide administrative layer.

In 1996 EDC replaced the old Ethiopian international boundary with the international boundary from the Digital Chart of the World (DCW).

The Ethiopian country specific sub-national boundaries were then integrated into the DCW master country database using the ARC/INFO command UPDATE.

The country specific data set, containing all of the sub-national hierarchy, was extracted from the DCW master data set using the ARC/INFO command RESELECT and the fourth level administrative units were extracted from the country specific data set using the ARC/INFO command DISSOLVE.

A master data set, based on the Digital Chart of the World (DCW) PONET layer, was created. Burkina Faso, Mali, Ethiopia, and Eritrea are included in the master data set, however, these countries have international boundaries that have been revised.
